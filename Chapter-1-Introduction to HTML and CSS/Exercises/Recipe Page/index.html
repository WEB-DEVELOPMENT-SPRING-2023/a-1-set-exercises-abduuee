Cinnamon Roll

A cinnamon roll is a big, fluffy, soft, and absolutely delicious sweet pastry that is filled with a cinnamon-sugar mixture and rolled into a spiral shape. It is then sliced into individual portions and baked until golden brown. Cinnamon rolls are often topped with a sweet glaze and are a popular breakfast pastry.

Ingredients

  • Flour - 5 cups
  • Sugar - 75g
  • Yeast - 4 tsp
  • Salt - 1 tsp
  • Butter - 85g
  • Egg - 1 whole
  • Cinnamon - 1 tbsp
  • Vanilla Extract - 2 tsp
  • Powdered Sugar - 120g
  • Cream Cheese

Method

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 2 cups flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. In a heat-safe bowl, combine the water and butter. Heat in the microwave for 30 sec - 45 sec. The butter should not melt completely.
  3. Pour into the dry ingredients with the egg and mix with a wooden spoon.
  4. Add in 2 more cups of flour and mix. It should turn pretty thick and sticky at this point.
  5. Once it reaches that point, set the spoon to the side and use your hands to mix and knead the dough.
  6. It will be sticky, so add another 1/4 cup of flour and continue to mix and knead by hand. It should turn into a smooth mass that's soft and tacky.
  7. With a clean finger, press it into the dough. If your finger is sticking, add another 1/4 cup of flour and knead. If it's not, then shape it into a ball and let it rest uncovered for 10 minutes.
  8. Place the dough on a lightly floured surface and pat it into a rough rectangle shape.
  9. Spread the butter into a thin and even layer, leaving about 1/2 inch border all around the outside of the dough.
  10. Sprinkle with the brown sugar and spread it even with your hand. Then top it with the cinnamon.
  11. Roll up the dough into a log. Place your hands at each end of the log and give it a gentle squeeze in to compact the log of dough. It may have stretched out a bit during the rolling process so this brings it back together.
  12. Using a knife, cut off the two ends of the log and then cut the remainder into 12 pieces.
  13. Place the rolls in a buttered or greased dish. It's OK if all of the rolls are touching.
  14. Place in a warm spot and cover with a towel to rise for 1 hour.
  15. Preheat the oven to 350F and check on the rolls. They should have doubled in size and now take up the entire dish.
  16. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the tops are a light golden brown. While they cool, make the icing.
  17. Place the cream cheese and butter in a bowl and use a fork to mash the two together. Make sure they're both softened to room temperature.
  18. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla and mash again with the fork. Once the mixture starts to loosen, switch to a whisk and mix until smooth.
  19. Spread onto the warm rolls and dig in!
